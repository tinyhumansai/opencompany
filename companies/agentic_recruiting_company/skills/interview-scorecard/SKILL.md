# Interview Scorecard

Run a consistent first-round interview and turn it into a scored, comparable
record — not a gut feeling.

## When to use

- A candidate advances to a first-round conversation.
- You need interviews that compare fairly across candidates.

## Steps

1. **Map** the role to three or four competencies to assess.
2. **Prepare** one behavioral question per competency.
3. **Run** the interview, capturing evidence, not impressions.
4. **Score** each competency against the [[Screening rubric]].
5. **Recommend** advance, hold, or pass with the evidence behind it.

## Output

A per-candidate scorecard: competency scores, supporting evidence, and an
advance / hold / pass call. The operator makes the final decision.
